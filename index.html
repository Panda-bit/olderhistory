Pre-European era
Indigenous tribes lived in the region now known as Brazil, with the largest group being the Tupi-Guarani peoples, who played a big role in shaping Brazil's cultural heritage.
1500
Portuguese navigator Pedro Álvares Cabral arrives in Brazil and claims the territory for Portugal. By arriving, he changed the future of the land (now Brazil) by inviting the Portugese to set up a government there.
1532
São Vicente was founded in 1532 by Portuguese colonizers, becoming the first official town in Brazil. This sets up a foundation for early Brazil to start building the towns and cities that make up the country today.
1693
The sugar industry becomes the main economic engine of Brazil, with sugar plantations established along the Northeast coast. By becoming a major part of the country's economics, this means the export of this product will determine a large part of Brazil's economy.
1763
Portugal transfers the Portuguese colony of Portugal and its territories in Brazil to the Crown of Portugal, creating a central way of controlling the colony.
1822
Brazil declares independence from Portugal, with Dom Pedro I crowned as the first emperor of the new nation. This is part of a large part of South America's fight for independence from Europeans.
1888
Slavery is abolished in Brazil. This frees many slaves from inhumane treatment and forced labor.
1889
A republic is established after the fall of the monarchy, with the first president being Deodoro da Fonseca. This marks the big transitional of policital ideology.
1930
Getúlio Vargas comes to power and establishes a dictatorship, known as the Estado Novo. This becomes a time when freedom of speech is illegal, and people lived under a very strict government.
1945
Vargas is overthrown and a democratic government is established.This marks the end of the dictatorship and more civilian freedom.
